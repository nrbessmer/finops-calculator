Cost Modeling • Live Cloud Pricing • Forecasting • Simulation • Exportable Reports

Overview

Cloud computing offers scalable, on‑demand resources—but its dynamic pricing and tiered discount models can lead to unexpected expenses. FinOps++ helps you build detailed cost models and forecasts for strategic budgeting.

Break down your spend into compute, storage, network, and licensing costs. Advanced settings let you simulate auto‑scaling and dynamic pricing adjustments. With forecasting, Monte Carlo simulations, and sensitivity analysis, you gain actionable insights to prevent budget overruns.

Practical Use Cases

Scenario: As the CFO of "CloudSavvy Retail," a marketing campaign triggers a 300% surge in traffic that overwhelms your cloud resources.

  1. Data Entry: Enter fixed costs and revenue assumptions to establish a baseline.
  2. Cost Modeling: Input detailed costs for compute, storage, network, and licensing using tiered discounts.
  3. Advanced Settings: Adjust auto‑scaling and dynamic pricing parameters to simulate cost‑saving strategies.
  4. Calculations & Forecasting: Run deterministic calculations and 12‑month forecasts to gauge financial impact.
  5. Simulations & Sensitivity: Use Monte Carlo simulations and sensitivity analysis to quantify risk and identify key cost drivers.

Outcome: With clear insights, you renegotiate discounts, optimize resource allocation, and adjust forecasts to prevent future overages.

Variable Explanations
  • Overall Fixed Cost ($): Baseline constant expenses.
  • Base Revenue per Unit ($): Revenue per unit at standard rates.
  • Revenue Units: Total units consumed.
  • Elasticity Threshold: Usage level where revenue per unit declines.
  • Elasticity Factor: Decrease in revenue per additional unit beyond threshold.
  • Cost Categories: Fixed and variable costs for compute, storage, network, and licensing.
  • Monthly Growth Rate: Expected increase in usage and costs month-over-month.
  • Simulation Iterations: Number of runs in Monte Carlo simulation.
  • Cost & Revenue Variability: Expected fluctuations due to market conditions.
